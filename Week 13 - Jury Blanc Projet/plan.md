# Marketing Data Analysis Plan

## Objective
As a Junior Data Analyst, my goal is to evaluate the effectiveness of recent marketing campaigns and identify the highest-performing product categories. I will analyze customer demographics, transactional behavior, and campaign responses to generate actionable business insights.

---

## 1. Core Business Questions (Questions Métier)
These are the fundamental questions my analysis must answer to provide value to the company:

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Who are our most valuable customers?** (How do income, marital status, education, and age relate to total spending?)
> **Which products drive the most revenue?** (Wines, Meats, Sweets, etc.)
> **Which marketing channels are most successful?** (Web vs. Catalog vs. Physical Store)
> **How effective are our marketing campaigns?** (Which campaign had the best conversion rate, and what is the profile of a customer who accepts a campaign?)

---

## 2. Phase 1: Data Preparation & Feature Engineering
Before analyzing, I must create new columns (features) to make the data easier to interpret.

### Tasks
- **Calculate Age**: Subtract `Year_Birth` from the current year to get the customer's age.
- **Calculate Total Spending**: Sum `MntWines`, `MntFruits`, `MntMeatProducts`, `MntFishProducts`, `MntSweetProducts`, and `MntBakedProds`.
- **Calculate Total Dependents**: Sum `Kidhome` and `Teenhome`.
- **Calculate Total Campaigns Accepted**: Sum all `AcceptedCmp` columns + the `Response` column.
- **Customer Tenure**: Calculate how many days/years they have been a customer based on `Dt_Customer`.

### Why do this?
Raw data is often difficult to plot. By calculating "Total Spending," I can easily create one chart to see who spends the most, rather than creating 6 separate charts for every single product category.

---

## 3. Phase 2: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

### Task 2A: Demographics Profiling (Who are our customers?)
- **Graphs**: 
  1. Boxplot of `Income` by `Education` level.
  2. Histogram of customer `Age`.
  3. Bar plot of customers by `Marital_Status`.
- **Why**: I need to establish a baseline of who shops with my company. Are the customers primarily young singles or older families?

### Task 2B: Product Purchasing Habits (What do they buy?)
- **Graphs**: 
  1. Pie chart or Bar chart showing the Total Revenue generated by each product category (Wines vs. Meats vs. Fruits).
  2. Scatter plot of `Income` vs `Total Spending`.
- **Why**: Identifying the most purchased products tells the marketing team what inventory to push in future advertisements. 

---

## 4. Phase 3: Campaign & Channel Effectiveness

### Task 3A: Evaluating Channels (Where do they buy?)
- **Graphs**: 
  1. Bar chart comparing total `NumWebPurchases`, `NumCatalogPurchases`, and `NumStorePurchases`.
  2. Scatter plot comparing `NumWebVisitsMonth` vs `NumWebPurchases` (Does visiting the website more often lead to more purchases?).
- **Why**: To optimize the budget. If catalog sales are extremely low compared to web purchases, the company should shift money into digital marketing rather than paper catalogs.

### Task 3B: Campaign Success (Do our ads work?)
- **Graphs**: 
  1. Bar chart comparing the conversion rate of Campaign 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and the latest 'Response' campaign.
  2. Boxplot of `Income` grouped by whether they `AcceptedCmp1` (to see if campaign 1 attracted wealthy customers).
- **Why**: Determining which campaign was most successful allows the marketing team to replicate its design and messaging in the future.

---

## 5. Phase 4: Insights & Final Conclusions
Once the graphs are rendered, I will write a summary answering the Business Questions.

### Example Conclusion Structure
1. **The Ideal Customer Profile**: Detail exactly who spends the most (e.g., "Married individuals aged 40-55 with advanced degrees and no teenagers at home").
2. **Product Focus**: Highlight the cash cows (e.g., "Wine accounts for 45% of all revenue, while sweets account for only 2%").
3. **Actionable Recommendation**: Give the company a next step based on the data (e.g., "Since Campaign 3 was highly successful among high-income earners, we should deploy a similar email marketing campaign featuring premium Wines next quarter.")
